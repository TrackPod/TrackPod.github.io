Features of the TrackPod

Target Selection

The TrackPod can center up to 3 targets in the camera frame to ensure everyone is the center of attention.

Portability

The TrackPod is battery powered and lightweight so you can snap photos on the go.

Dual-Axis Motion

The TrackPod uses a dual motor system to track targets vertically and horizontally.

Wireless Connectivity

TrackPod uses wireless connectivity to eliminate the need for cables.

Low Entry Price

The TrackPod starts at $600 CAD, nearly $300 cheaper than competing products on the market.

Universal Phone Mount

The TrackPod uses a universal phone mount so you and your friends can share the TrackPod without needing adapters or dongles.

Elegant Product Design

A well designed product means more time taking photos and less time reading documentation. Plus, it's nice to look at.

  • Sleek and streamlined look constructed with high quality materials.

Project Updates

Update 1

Jan 16, 2020

The pitch and yaw brackets were 3D printed and a test fixture was made to help increase developing speed. All the 3D printed parts use high quality PLA filament for strength and robustness.

Update 2

Jan 24, 2020

The battery pack was made and voltage tests were done with the BMS and buck converter. Some more development was made with the embedded controller and HTTP tests were done on an Android app.

Update 3

Jan 28, 2020

The HTTP request tests were run with the system. A demonstrative video was taken to capture the servo movements being controlled wirelessly via the Android app. Next steps at this point are delving deeper into the computer vision functionality of the app and performing tests with the batteries and a mounted phone. The HTTP test video can be seen here.

Update 4

Feb 5, 2020

Initial testing was performed on the Android app. The app uses machine learning facial recognition techniques to match detected faces with the closest matching candidate based on downloaded classifiers. In the future, classifiers for the group members will be extended and functionality will be added to support in-app training.

Update 5

Feb 18, 2020

Analytics were added via a debug option in the application. This improves the testing process and exposes more information on the screen, allowing the operator to troubleshoot issues and see how the classification decision is being made.

Update 6

Feb 21, 2020

The mobile application now supports in-app classifier training. Users can import pictures of the subject to add them as a custom target.

Update 7 Pt. 1

Feb 26, 2020

Completed the connectivity between the mobile application and the ESP32 microcontroller. As in the HTTP testing, requests are used to move the mount to a target position based on the positional info in the camera frame.

Update 7 Pt. 2

Feb 26, 2020

Calibrated the new glass build plate on the 3D printer, which was purchased to improve build consistency prior to the final print of the TrackPod chassis.

Update 8

Feb 28, 2020

Integration of the electrical, mechanical, and software systems was tested. Video of the combined system after troubleshooting can be seen here.

Update 9

Mar 1, 2020

Completed the chassis base design and began 3D printing. The electronics were mounted in the middle chassis section and connected with the servos on the top. Updates to the image capture sections of the code were implemented. Functionality was added to include the tilt axis in the tracking motion.

Update 10

Mar 3, 2020

The TrackPod assembly was mounted on the tripod and full integration tests were performed for robustness and consistency. A brief demonstrative video of one of the tracking and image capture test can be seen here.

Update 11

Mar 5, 2020

The group centering feature was implemented and tested with 4 people in the camera frame. All the faces in the photo are detected and the algorithm determines the optimal position for the camera to be in to capture all subjects equally.
